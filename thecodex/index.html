The Codex

Ideas Before Specifications

The Codex is a living record of working ideas, unresolved questions, and evolving concepts. It does not finalize BitPangea. It preserves the exploration before the rules harden.

Codex Principle This document does not define the world. It records the search for what the world may become.

Working Ideas

Concepts that may shape BitPangea but remain open to refinement.

Open Questions

Problems not yet solved, because premature answers can become future constraints.

Deferred Decisions

Choices intentionally delayed until the world is ready for stronger commitments.

Current Working Concepts

  • One World: BitPangea is imagined as a single unified digital supercontinent.
  • 21 Million Parcels: The world is constrained by a finite parcel supply.
  • No Expansion: The map should not be arbitrarily enlarged once defined.
  • No Duplication: The world should not be copied into competing official versions.
  • No Authority: No central power should be able to alter the map on a whim.
  • Built to Endure: The project should be designed for long-term permanence rather than short-term spectacle.

Open Questions

  • How should parcels be created, discovered, or assigned?
  • What determines the geometry of a parcel?
  • How should the world be mapped before ownership exists?
  • What technical system can preserve ownership without central control?
  • Should BitPangea use an existing blockchain, a layered approach, or another structure entirely?
  • How should governance emerge without compromising the founding constraints?
  • What belongs permanently in the world, and what should remain outside it?

Historical Concepts

Several ideas have already become important historical reference points during the first days of BitPangea.

  • The Atlas Principle: Before ownership can be claimed, the world must first be mapped.
  • Genesis Parcel (Lot 0): A permanently reserved monument to the origins of BitPangea.
  • The First Stone: A preserved record of the world's earliest public appearance.
  • 21 Epoch Model: A possible future release structure involving 21 epochs of 1,000,000 parcels each.

Deferred Decisions

BitPangea is intentionally avoiding premature commitments in areas where the wrong decision could define the world too early.

  • Blockchain selection
  • Parcel issuance mechanics
  • Ownership transfer rules
  • Governance structure
  • Economic model
  • Map generation and parcel geometry
  • Long-term stewardship and succession

What The Codex Is Not

The Codex is not a whitepaper, protocol specification, roadmap, investment document, or promise of future implementation.

It is a record of inquiry. It exists so future builders can understand not only what decisions were made, but what questions existed before the decisions.

For Future Readers If this record survives, let it show that BitPangea began not with answers, but with restraint.
