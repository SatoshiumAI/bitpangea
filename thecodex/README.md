# BitPangea Codex

## Working Record

### Ideas Before Specifications

The Codex is a living record of working ideas, unresolved questions, and evolving concepts.

It does not finalize BitPangea.

It preserves the exploration before the rules harden.

---

> **Codex Principle**
>
> This document does not define the world. It records the search for what the world may become.

---

## Purpose

The Codex exists to preserve inquiry.

It records:

- Working ideas
- Open questions
- Historical concepts
- Deferred decisions
- Areas intentionally left unresolved

The Codex values exploration over premature certainty.

---

## Current Working Concepts

### One World
BitPangea is imagined as a single unified digital supercontinent.

### 21 Million Parcels
The world is constrained by a finite parcel supply.

### No Expansion
The map should not be arbitrarily enlarged once defined.

### No Duplication
The world should not be copied into competing official versions.

### No Authority
No central power should be able to alter the map on a whim.

### Built to Endure
The project should be designed for long-term permanence rather than short-term spectacle.

---

## Open Questions

Some questions remain intentionally unanswered:

- How should parcels be created, discovered, or assigned?
- What determines the geometry of a parcel?
- How should the world be mapped before ownership exists?
- What technical system can preserve ownership without central control?
- Should BitPangea use an existing blockchain, a layered approach, or another structure entirely?
- How should governance emerge without compromising the founding constraints?
- What belongs permanently in the world, and what should remain outside it?

---

## Historical Concepts

Several ideas have become recurring reference points:

- The Atlas Principle
- Genesis Parcel (Lot 0)
- The First Stone
- The 21 Epoch Model

These concepts are preserved because they help explain the evolution of BitPangea's early thinking.

---

## Deferred Decisions

BitPangea intentionally avoids premature commitments in areas where early decisions could permanently shape the world.

Examples include:

- Blockchain selection
- Parcel issuance mechanics
- Ownership transfer rules
- Governance structure
- Economic model
- Map generation and parcel geometry
- Long-term stewardship and succession

---

## What The Codex Is Not

The Codex is not:

- A whitepaper
- A protocol specification
- A roadmap
- An investment document
- A promise of future implementation

It exists so future builders can understand not only what decisions were made, but what questions existed before those decisions.
