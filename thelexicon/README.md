# The Lexicon

## Preserved Vocabulary

### The Language of BitPangea

The Lexicon exists to preserve the evolving language of BitPangea.

Some words describe institutions.
Some describe geography.
Others describe ideas that do not yet have final answers.

Definitions may evolve.

The language itself becomes part of the record.

---

> **Foundational Principle**
>
> Civilizations are built not only from territory, but from shared language.

---

## Purpose

The Lexicon preserves the vocabulary used to describe BitPangea, its institutions, geography, records, artifacts, and civilizational concepts.

It exists so future readers can understand not only what the world contained, but how it was described.

---

## Major Categories

### Geography

Terms describing the physical and cartographic structure of the world, including:

- World
- Supercontinent
- Parcel
- Atlas
- Frontier
- Region

### Institutions

Terms describing the major institutions of BitPangea, including:

- Charter
- Codex
- Chronicle
- Archive
- Commons
- Covenant

### Records and Artifacts

Terms describing preserved records, monuments, signals, and historical objects, including:

- Transmission
- Signal
- Reliquary
- Lot 0
- First Stone
- The Wall

### Civilization

Terms describing relationships, responsibilities, and social structures, including:

- Claim
- Ownership
- Settlement
- Stewardship
- Inheritance
- Governance

### Historical Periods

Terms describing eras of development and exploration, including:

- Foundation Era
- Cartographic Era
- Discovery Era
- Claiming Era

---

## Relationship To Other Institutions

The Lexicon serves a distinct role within the BitPangea institutional framework:

- The Charter preserves principles.
- The Codex preserves inquiry.
- The Chronicle preserves history.
- The Archive preserves knowledge.
- The Commons preserves discussion.
- The Frontier Office preserves communication.
- The Lexicon preserves language.

---

## Preservation Philosophy

Words matter.

Language shapes how civilizations understand territory, ownership, history, memory, governance, and inheritance.

The Lexicon exists to preserve continuity of meaning even as definitions evolve over time.

---

> The language remains unfinished.
>
> The record remains open.
