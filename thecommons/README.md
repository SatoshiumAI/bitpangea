# The Commons

## Public Gathering

### Questions From the Edge of a New World

The Commons is the public gathering place of BitPangea.

It exists to preserve thoughtful questions, criticism, exploration, and discussion while the world remains in its earliest stages.

---

## Purpose

The Commons exists as a place where inquiry can gather.

Before there are institutions, markets, settlements, or ownership systems, there must be a place where ideas can be examined and challenged.

The Commons encourages:

- Questions
- Criticism
- Exploration
- Research
- Discussion
- Long-term thinking

---

## What Belongs In The Commons

- Questions about scarce digital geography
- Thoughtful criticism of the BitPangea concept
- Ideas concerning ownership, permanence, and governance
- Long-term civilization and worldbuilding questions
- Historical parallels and research
- Questions that help define what should never change

---

## What Does Not Belong

- Token requests
- Investment solicitations
- Parcel reservations
- Land sale inquiries
- Speculative price discussions
- Spam, promotion, or hype

BitPangea remains an exploratory concept.

No parcels exist.
No allocations exist.
No sales exist.

---

## Foundational Questions

Several questions help define the purpose of The Commons:

- Why should digital land be scarce?
- What does it mean to own a parcel?
- What rules should never change?
- Can a digital world have a constitutional core?
- What kind of civilization might emerge from finite digital geography?

---

## Relationship To Other Institutions

The Commons serves a different role from the other major BitPangea institutions:

- The Charter preserves principles.
- The Codex preserves inquiry.
- The Chronicle preserves history.
- The Archive preserves knowledge.
- The Commons preserves discussion.

---

> Before there are cities, markets, or maps, there must be a place where questions can gather.
